Nigeria: What Everyone Needs to Know® Paperback

$13.90

Category: Books
  • Description
  • Reviews (0)

As the “Giant of Africa” Nigeria is home to about twenty percent of Sub-Saharan Africa’s population, serves as the largest oil and gas producer in Africa, comprises the largest economy in Africa, and represents the cultural center of African literature, film, and music. Nevertheless, the country is afflicted by problems that prevent it from realizing its potential as a global power. Boko Haram, a radical Islamist insurgency based in the country’s northeast, is an ongoing security threat, as is the growing turmoil in the Niger Delta, the heartland of Nigeria’s petroleum riches. There is also continuing violence linked to land and water use, ethnicity and religion.

 

In Nigeria: What Everyone Needs to Know®, John Campbell and Matthew Page provide a rich contemporary overview of this crucial African country. Delving into Nigeria’s recent history, politics, and culture, this volume tackles essential questions related to widening inequality, the historic 2015 presidential election, the persistent security threat of Boko Haram, rampant government corruption, human rights concerns, and the continual conflicts that arise in a country that is roughly half Christian and half Muslim.

 

Nigeria’s success as a democracy is in the fundamental interest of its African neighbors, the United States, and the international community, with its continent-wide influence in a host of areas. This book will provide an accessible, unique overview of the country to interested readers.
